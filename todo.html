To Do in NL
Beautiful Scenery, Rich History and Heritage

Railway Coastal Museum

The Railway Coastal Museum is a transport museum located in St. John's. It is located in the historic Newfoundland Railway terminal on Water Street and contains exhibits detailing the history of the Newfoundland Railway and the history of coastal water transportation in the province.


495 Water Street

St. John's

L'anse Aux Meadows

L'Anse aux Meadows is an archaeological site, first excavated in the 1960s, of a Norse settlement dating to approximately 1,000 years ago. The site is located on the northernmost tip of the island of Newfoundland, near St. Anthony.


St. Anthony

Newfoundland and Labrador

Johnson Geo Centre

The Johnson Geo Centre is a geological interpretation centre located on Signal Hill in St. John's. The museum is named for philanthropist Paul Johnson, and was opened in 2002.


175 Signal Hill Road

St. John's

Cape Spear Lighthouse

Cape Spear is a dramatic Atlantic shoreline, fringed in long headlands and fjords. This site includes views of whales and icebergs, exploration of a historic coastal fortress, and a national landmark - The Cape Spear Lighthouse.


Blackhead Road

St. John's

The Rooms

The Rooms is a museum dedicated to natural & cultural history in Newfoundland. It is a modern facility and includes a gallery of local art.


9 Bonaventure Avenue

St. John's

East Coast Trail

The East Coast Trail is a long-distance coastal footpath. It is a developed trail over 336 kilometres long, creation of which began in 1994. It is made up of 25 linked wilderness paths and passes through more than 30 communities in Newfoundland.


Various Locations

Newfoundland and Labrador

The Basilica Cathedral of St. John the Baptist

The Basilica-Cathedral of St. John the Baptist in St. John's, Newfoundland and Labrador is the metropolitan cathedral of the Roman Catholic Archdiocese of St. John's. It is the mother church and symbol of Roman Catholicism in Newfoundland.


200 Military Road

St. John's

Signal Hill and Cabot Tower

Cabot Tower is in St. John's, situated on Signal Hill. Construction of the tower began in 1898 to commemorate the 400th anniversary of John Cabot's landing in Newfoundland, and Queen Victoria's Diamond Jubilee.


Signal Hill Rd

St. John's
